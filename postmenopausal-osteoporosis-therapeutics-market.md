<p><p>The postmenopausal osteoporosis therapeutics market encompasses various treatment options aimed at reducing fracture risk. Bisphosphonates inhibit bone resorption, promoting bone density. Hormones, such as estrogen therapy, help maintain bone mass. Strontium ranelate reduces bone loss and stimulates new bone formation. Raloxifene, a selective estrogen receptor modulator, offers similar benefits without some hormone-related risks. Denosumab, a monoclonal antibody, targets osteoclasts to decrease bone resorption. Other therapies may include calcitonin and antifracture agents, providing diverse treatment strategies for managing osteoporosis.</p></p>
