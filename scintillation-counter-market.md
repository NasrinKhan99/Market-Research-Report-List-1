Organic crystals are known for their high light output and fast decay times, making them suitable for medical imaging. Inorganic crystals, such as sodium iodide, offer excellent energy resolution and are widely used in radiation detection. Polymer phosphors provide flexibility and adaptability, making them ideal for portable devices and applications requiring lightweight materials. Each type caters to specific applications within radiation detection and measurement.</p></p>
