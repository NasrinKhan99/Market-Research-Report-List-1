<p><p>The CPU heatsink market features three primary cooling types: air cooling, water cooling, and thermoelectric cooling. Air cooling utilizes heat sinks and fans to dissipate heat through airflow, offering affordability and simplicity. Water cooling employs coolant-filled systems to transfer heat away more efficiently, ideal for high-performance setups. Thermoelectric cooling uses Peltier modules to create a temperature differential, providing precise thermal management but requiring additional power and complexity. Each type caters to varying user needs, from casual to advanced gaming and computing.</p></p>
