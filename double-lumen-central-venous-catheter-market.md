<p><p>The double-lumen central venous catheter market comprises various types based on the insertion site. Jugular vein catheters are inserted into the jugular vein, offering easy access for emergency situations. Subclavian vein catheters are placed under the collarbone, preferred for long-term use due to lower infection risk. Femoral vein catheters are inserted in the thigh, ideal for quick access but associated with higher infection rates. Other types may include peripheral inserted central catheters (PICC) and specialized designs for specific clinical needs.</p></p>
