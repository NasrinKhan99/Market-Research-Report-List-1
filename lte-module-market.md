<p><ul><li>EPC (Evolved Packet Core)</li><li>ENode B (Evolved Node B)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The LTE module market encompasses two key components: EPC (Evolved Packet Core) and ENode B (Evolved Node B). EPC is the backbone of LTE networks, managing data routing and connectivity, while ENode B refers to the base stations that facilitate wireless communication between user devices and the core network. Together, these elements support high-speed mobile data transmission, low latency, and seamless connectivity, driving the adoption of LTE technology across various sectors, including telecommunications, IoT, and smart cities.</p></p>
