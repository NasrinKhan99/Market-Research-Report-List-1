<p><p>Fire alarm and detection systems are crucial for safety and come in two primary types: conventional and addressable systems. Conventional fire detection systems divide areas into zones, triggering alarms when smoke or heat is detected in a specific zone, making them simpler but less precise. Addressable fire detection systems, on the other hand, provide individual identification for each device, allowing for more accurate location tracking and data reporting, enhancing response capabilities and overall safety in various environments.</p></p>
