<p><p>The multiparticle medical cyclotron market is divided into two primary types: low energy medical cyclotrons and high energy medical cyclotrons. Low energy medical cyclotrons, typically operating below 20 MeV, are used for producing radioisotopes for diagnostic imaging and treatment in nuclear medicine. In contrast, high energy medical cyclotrons, exceeding 20 MeV, are designed for advanced applications, including the production of therapeutic isotopes and proton therapy for cancer treatment, offering enhanced capabilities for patient care and research.</p></p>
